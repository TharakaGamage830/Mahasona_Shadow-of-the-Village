# 🏮 Yaksha Village: The Grimoire of Souls

**Yaksha Village** is a social deduction game of survival, strategy, and ancient Sri Lankan folklore. In a remote village haunted by the shadow of the **Mahasona**, players must work together to identify the demon hiding among them—or perish in the darkness.

---

## 📜 The Legend (Story)
For generations, the village has lived in peace, protected by ancient rituals. But the seal has weakened. The **Mahasona**, a legendary giant hunter of souls, has possessed one of the villagers. By day, he looks like a neighbor. By night, he hunts. 

The **Vedamahattaya** (The Storyteller) has gathered the souls for a final **Thovil** ritual. You have until the last villager falls to find the demon and burn him at the stake in the **Gam Maduwa** (Tribunal).

---

## 🎭 The Cast of Souls (Roles)

### 👹 The Evil Team (The Darkness)
Their goal: Eliminate the villagers until they are equal in number to the survivors.

*   **Mahasona (The Demon)**: The alpha hunter. Every night, he chooses one soul to harvest. If he is the last one standing (or is tied with the village), the village is consumed by darkness.
*   **Riri Yaka (The Minion/Poisoner)**: A chaotic spirit who corrupts minds. Each night, he picks a player to "Poison." If that player has a special power, their power will give a **false result** or fail that night.
*   **Kalu Kumaraya (The Minion/Swapper)**: A trickster of space and time. He can swap the physical seats of two players each night, causing investigators to look at the wrong people or healers to protect the wrong souls.

### 🕯️ The Townsfolk (The Good/Protectors)
Their goal: Identify and execute the Mahasona at the Tribunal.

*   **Kattandiya (The Investigator)**: The ritual specialist. Each night, he can look into the soul of one player. The spirits will show him **RED** if the player is Evil or **WHITE** if they are Good. 
    *   *Note: If poisoned by Riri Yaka, his vision is reversed!*
*   **Pirith Monk (The Healer)**: The spiritual shield. He can choose one player to protect each night. That player cannot be killed by the Mahasona.
    *   *Lore: The Monk cannot protect himself—self-sacrifice is his path.*
*   **Vedda Hunter (The Warrior)**: A marksman who never misses. If the Mahasona kills the Hunter at night, the Hunter takes one last shot and kills another player of his choice before dying.
*   **Gama Ralahamy (The Village Head)**: A leader with a powerful voice. During the day's vote, his word counts for **1.5x** more than a regular villager's.
*   **Standard Townsfolk**: Humble villagers with no magic, but they hold the power of the communal vote.

### 🍱 The Outsiders (The Cursed)
They are technically on the **Good** team, but their existence makes the game harder for everyone.

*   **Pretaya (The Hungry Ghost)**: A weak, cursed soul. His voice is barely a whisper, so his vote only counts as **0.5x** at the Tribunal.

---

## 🌑 The Gameplay Cycle

### 1. The Night (The Hunt)
The village falls silent. One by one, the **Vedamahattaya** wakes the spirits:
1.  **Minions**: Recognize each other and the Mahasona.
2.  **Kalu Kumaraya**: Swaps two seats.
3.  **Riri Yaka**: Selects a player to poison.
4.  **Kattandiya**: Investigates one player.
5.  **Pirith Monk**: Protects one player.
6.  **Mahasona**: Kills one player.

### 2. The Day (The Gam Maduwa)
The sun rises, and the village discovers who was lost to the night.
*   **Discussion**: Survivors debate, share visions, and cast suspicion.
*   **Nomination**: Any player can nominate another to face the fire.
*   **The Vote**: The village votes "Burn" or "Spare." If the majority votes to Burn, the player is executed, and their role is revealed.

---

## ⚖️ Game Logic & Balance
The ritual's difficulty scales with the number of gathered souls (5-20 players):

| Players | Demon | Minions | Outsiders | Townsfolk |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **5-6** | 1 | 1 | 1 | 2-3 |
| **7-9** | 1 | 2 | 1 | 3-5 |
| **10-12** | 1 | 2 | 2 | 5-7 |
| **13-15** | 1 | 3 | 2 | 7-9 |
| **16-20** | 1 | 3 | 3 | 9-13 |

### 🏆 How to Win
*   **The Village Wins**: If the **Mahasona** is executed at the Tribunal.
*   **The Darkness Wins**: If the **Mahasona** is still alive and the number of living Evil players equals or exceeds the number of living Good players.
