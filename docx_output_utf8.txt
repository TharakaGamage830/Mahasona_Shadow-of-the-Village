1. The Horror Vision

Yaksha Gama is not a lighthearted game with demon costumes. It is a game of dread, paranoia, and ritualistic terror rooted in genuine Sri Lankan nightmare mythology. Every mechanic is designed to make players feel the oppressive horror of a real Thovil exorcism gone wrong ΓÇö the torches guttering, the drumbeats wrong, the sweat of not knowing who among your circle has been possessed.




1.1 Three Pillars of Horror




  PILLAR 1: BODY HORROR ΓÇö The demons are grotesque. Mahasona has a backward bear skull for a head. Riri Yaka is a blood-drenched monkey-faced pygmy who stands on your chest as you die. These visuals must be present in every art asset.




  PILLAR 2: SOCIAL PARANOIA ΓÇö You cannot trust anyone. Your closest ally could be Kalu Kumaraya, wearing a human face, whispering lies in your ear. The horror is not a monster in the dark ΓÇö it is your best friend smiling at you.




  PILLAR 3: RITUAL DREAD ΓÇö The game is framed as a real Thovil ceremony that has gone wrong. The Vedamahattaya is an exorcist who has lost control. Players are not playing a game ΓÇö they are participants in a failed demon binding.








2. Mythological Accuracy ΓÇö Demon Profiles

Every role in Yaksha Gama uses the authentic mythological description from Sinhala oral tradition and documented folklore. These are not invented. They are real.

3.3 Win Condition Clarity

The good team wins ONLY by executing Mahasona during the day phase. There is no other path to victory.



The evil team wins the MOMENT only 2 living players remain ΓÇö even if Mahasona was nominated for execution that same day.



Critical ordering: If a day ends with an execution that reduces living players to exactly 2, and the executed player was NOT Mahasona ΓÇö evil wins before any new vote can be called. The Vedamahattaya must count living players after every execution before proceeding.



Tie vote scenario: If a nomination vote ends in a perfect tie, NO execution happens that day. This is not a bug ΓÇö it is a feature. Mahasona benefits from indecision. Evil teams should always try to split votes.

Pirith Thread ΓÇö Actual white thread (as used in Sri Lankan rituals) tied to the protected player&apos;s wrist. Physically present.


Role Tokens ΓÇö Carved wooden tokens, not cards. Each demon face matches the traditional Ambalangoda Kolam mask design for that specific demon.

7. 3D Asset Pipeline ΓÇö Step by Step

Use this pipeline to produce 3D assets for: game box packaging, Kickstarter campaign renders, collectible figurine add-ons, and a potential digital adaptation.




Phase 1 ΓÇö Concept to Mesh


Generate multiple Midjourney images of each demon from different angles using --cref to maintain consistency across views.


Upload the best front-and-back view to Meshy AI or Tripo to auto-generate a rough 3D mesh. This takes 2ΓÇô5 minutes per character.


Import rough mesh into ZBrush as a reference. Begin high-poly sculpt ΓÇö starting with the skull shape, then body proportions, then surface detail (fur, scales, wounds).


For Mahasona specifically: sculpt the bear skull head SEPARATELY from the neck, then attach at an anatomically wrong backward angle ΓÇö this is his defining horror trait.




Phase 2 ΓÇö Retopology and Rigging


Export high-poly ZBrush mesh. Import into Blender. Use Blender&apos;s Shrinkwrap retopology to create a clean 10,000ΓÇô20,000 poly game mesh.


Build an armature (skeleton) rig in Blender for each character. Even for a board game, rigged models allow you to quickly generate multiple pose renders for marketing.


For the Katawaliya box: model in Blender using Sri Lankan pethi petiya reference images. Add carved kolam mask reliefs on all four faces of the box.




Phase 3 ΓÇö Texturing


Bake normal maps from the high-poly ZBrush mesh to the retopologised Blender mesh. This transfers the fine detail without the polygon cost.


Import the game-ready mesh into Substance Painter. Paint all textures: Mahasona&apos;s blood-red skin with grey fur on the bear skull, Riri Yaka&apos;s monkey face with dried blood, Kalu Kumaraya&apos;s flawlessly smooth skin with a slight unnatural sheen.


Export PBR texture maps (Albedo, Normal, Roughness, Metallic). Import into Blender Cycles for final horror renders.




Phase 4 ΓÇö Horror Lighting & Render


In Blender Cycles: set up a scene with two Thovil torches (warm flickering orange), a night sky HDRI, and one ominous backlight in deep red.


Render at 4K minimum. Use Blender&apos;s denoising and Filmic colour profile for cinematic horror look.


For Kickstarter: render each demon in a dramatic pose against the Thovil ceremony background environment. Composite in Photoshop ΓÇö add smoke, sparks, blood splatter.
